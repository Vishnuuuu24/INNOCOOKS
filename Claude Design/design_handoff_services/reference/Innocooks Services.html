Reading this study

The hero's Thread was a vertical strand. Here it lies down and becomes a rail — the four things we build ride along it. Scroll to slide the rail; the centred card rises forward, turns to face you, and warms to gold as it crosses the focal point.

No seam, ever

There are no gradient bands. One fixed backdrop has its colour lerped in OKLab by scroll — dusk → parchment → dusk — and the melt happens inside empty scroll gaps, so no content is ever on screen while the colour shifts. Zero banding, zero visible jump.

One rail, all the way down

The Thread lives on a single fixed, full-page canvas. It floats faintly through the dusk lead-in, brightens and grows the focal bead on the parchment stage, then carries on into the next section — one element threading every “page” together, with the cards rising onto it as you arrive.

Warming — the woven idea, again

In the hero's "woven" mode the Thread warmed each letter it crossed. The same gesture returns: whichever card sits on the focal bead warms from --gold-deep to --gold, its border lights and it lifts toward you.

Real 3D, cheap

The depth is pure CSS 3D transforms on a perspective stage — no WebGL. The rail is one 2D canvas. Cursor tilts the whole scene a few degrees; the focal card always slides to centre.

Porting into Next

Replace the sticky stack in Services.tsx with a pinned ScrollTrigger driving one progress value p ∈ [0,3]; each card transform is a pure function of i − p. The backdrop melt + persistent rail run from a single fixed layer in layout.tsx so every section shares them. Cards stay real DOM (SEO + a11y intact).

Guardrails

Reduced-motion and narrow screens drop to a clean vertical stack of the same cards — no pin, no canvas. DPR capped at 2. The rail never gates the content: every word is readable with motion off.

Tweaks

Raise Card solidity to stop neighbouring cards bleeding through as they pass the focus.
