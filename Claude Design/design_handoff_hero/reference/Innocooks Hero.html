Reading this study

One hero, three directions — flip them with the switcher and move your cursor. Everything is real-time: the Thread is verlet physics, not a video.

Strand — material

The Thread becomes an actual hanging strand of light. Gravity + distance constraints give it weight; it sways on an ambient breeze and recoils from your cursor. A bead travels it like the end of a noodle being pulled.

Dusk — atmosphere

Depth and air. A lantern of light lags behind the cursor, dust drifts up at three parallax depths, and the wordmark's letters separate in space as you move.

Woven — type

The Thread laces over and under the letters of the name, warming each one to gold as it crosses. The brand motif and the wordmark become one object.

Why not Three.js

For one strand + atmosphere, 2D canvas wins: ~12 KB of JS, 60fps on a phone, no WebGL context to lose. Reserve WebGL for the day you want true volumetric light or a 3D product on a project page — not for this.

Porting into Next

Drop thread.js + atmosphere.js logic into a "use client" <HeroCanvas/>, start/stop the rAF in useEffect, and gate it behind prefers-reduced-motion (already wired here). The wordmark stays real DOM text for SEO; canvas sits behind it.

Guardrails

Reduced-motion renders one composed still. No pointer → the lantern drifts on its own. DPR capped at 2. Mobile keeps the strand, drops cursor forces. The hero never gates content behind the animation.
